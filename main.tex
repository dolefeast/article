\begin{abstract}
We study the backreaction of a charged quantum scalar field in the presence of two opposite charges placed at the boundaries of a finite one-dimensional region, with attention to boundary effects. We review previous work by Ambjørn \& Wolfram \cite{ambjorn_properties_1983} which finds, in this setup,  that vacuum polarization averts instabilities. 
As it was later pointed out, \cite{ambjorn_properties_1983} used a wrong prescription for computing the vacuum polarization \cite{schlemmer_current_2015}. The correct prescription presents notable discrepancies with the one previously used \cite{wernersson_vacuum_2021}. 
We find that using the correct prescription the system remains stable despite these discrepancies. To this end, an iterative procedure is introduced to study the system under varying mass and boundary conditions configurations.
\end{abstract}
