Using the iterative procedure above laid out we study two particular cases: The massless scalar field subject to DBC, and  $m=1$ with
the scalar field subject to NBC. We do not study the massless field subject to NBC, since under these boundary conditions, $\lambda_c=0$ for $m=0$, as pointed out in Ref.~\cite{wernersson_vacuum_2021}.

Figures~\ref{fig:main-results-dirichlet} and \ref{fig:main-results-neumann}  display the dynamics of the coupled Klein-Gordon-Maxwell system in the semiclassical approximation. The external field approximation is given for reference in orange. Notice the different $\lambda$ scales. The system is studied through the energies of the first modes (top panel), the induced charge $Q_I$  in the region $z\in[0,0.5]$ (middle panel) and the total electric field $\lambda+Q_I$ at the midpoint $z=0.5$ (bottom panel). We observe in both cases how considering backreaction avoids the instabilities reflected in $Q_I\to-\infty$ that the external field approximation presents. Indeed, the energy of the lower modes is raised which "prevents" $\omega_1$ from crossing 0. We also point the reader to notice how the rapid growth of $|Q_I|$ as $\lambda$ crosses $\lambda_c$ keeps $\lambda + Q_I$ below $\lambda_c$. 

One might also notice the almost identical behavior of the self-consistent solutions to the
external field approximation in the weak-field regime $\lambda \ll {\lambda_c}$ in both cases.

\subsection{Mode sum formula}






\begin{figure}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.45\textwidth]{Figures/dirichlet/inducedChargeComparison.pdf}
    \caption{$Q_I$ for the self-consistent solutions for a massless field subject to DBC, by using different expressions for $\rho$. In blue, the (correct) vacuum polarization obtained through renormalization through point-splitting w.r.t. a Hadamard parametrix, and in orange the vacuum polarization obtained through a mode sum formula truncated to one mode used in \cite{ambjorn_properties_1983}.}
    \label{fig:hambjorn-iterative}
\end{figure}


Fig.~\ref{fig:hambjorn-iterative} displays $Q_I$ in the iterative procedure as a function of $\lambda$ for two different prescriptions for $\rho$. In blue, the correct prescription used throughout this paper. In orange, $\rho$ as defined in \cite{ambjorn_properties_1983}, truncated to the first mode. Notice the different behaviors in the two regimes: although the truncated mode sum formula presents a noticeably stronger vacuum polarization in the weak-field regime, their strong-field behavior is very similar. A possible explanation is the fact that the main contribution to vacuum polarization comes from the first modes, which are of course those with less energy. Thus, in strong-field regime the main contribution to \eqref{eq:vacuum-polarization-long} comes from the first modes, and rendering a truncation to first modes a very good approximation.
