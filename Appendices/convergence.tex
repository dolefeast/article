The iterative procedure either
    converges,
    oscillates, or
    the appearance of complex $\omega$ cuts the sequence short.
    
A case where the sequence convergences, and oscillates is displayed in Fig.~\ref{fig:convergence}  in the top,  and bottom panel, respectively. We measure convergence in the frequency of the lowest mode $\omega_\lambda^\kappa$.
These convergence cases can be understood from the perspective of fixed-point theory. For brevity, we invite the reader to find the fixed points of the $\mathbb{R}\rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ functions $\frac{3}{4}-\frac{x}{2}, 1-x$ to see how the slope of the function affects convergence. The parameter $c$ in Eq.~\eqref{eq:A0-general-iterative} allows us to control this slope in order to ensure convergence.


Complex $\omega$ might appear if the candidate $\lambda +\Delta \lambda $ in the numerical continuation method is too far away from $\lambda$. The screening due to $\rho_\lambda$  is ``too weak'' compared to $A_{0, \lambda+\Delta \lambda}^1$, which makes the system effectively behave as in the external field approximation with $\lambda>\lambda_c$.
